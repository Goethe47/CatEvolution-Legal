Privacy Policy — Cat Evolution

Last updated: July 15, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information the mobile game Cat Evolution (package name com.goethe47.catevolution, developed by Goethe47, "we", "us") collects, how it is used, and who it is shared with. By installing or using the app, you agree to the practices described here.

1. Information We Collect

Cat Evolution itself does not ask you to create an account or type in any personal information (no name, email, or phone number). The data below is collected only through the third-party services built into the app, described in the table in Section 2.

  • Advertising identifiers & device data — when ads are shown, our advertising partner (Unity LevelPlay / Unity Ads) may collect your device's advertising ID (GAID), IP address, general (coarse) location derived from IP, device model, OS version, and ad interaction data (impressions, clicks), used to serve and measure ads.
  • Google Play Games account data — if you sign in with Google Play Games, Google shares your public Play Games profile (display name, avatar, player ID) with the app so we can show leaderboards and save your progress to your account.
  • Gameplay data — score, progress, and settings (e.g. sound on/off) are stored locally on your device, and in your Google Play Games save if you're signed in. This data is not sent to us directly.

3. Ad Personalization Choices

You can limit ad tracking on your device:

  • Android: Settings → Google → Ads → "Delete advertising ID" or "Opt out of Ads Personalization".

4. Data Retention

Locally stored gameplay data stays on your device until you uninstall the app or clear its data. Data held by Unity and Google is retained and deleted according to their own policies linked above.

5. Children's Privacy

Cat Evolution is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us using the details below so we can remove it.

6. Your Rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete data held about you by our third-party partners (Section 2) — these requests should generally be made directly to Google or Unity, since we do not hold that data ourselves. For anything you believe we hold directly, contact us below.
